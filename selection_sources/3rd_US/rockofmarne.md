It Wasn't Really About Paris
To this day it remains an article of faith in far too many history books [and Major Short's
presentation] that the Germans were attempting to attack Paris in July 1918. [A major objective of
the operation] was to once more try to force the Allies to pull the French reserves out of Flanders
by making it look like the real objective of the German attack was Paris. All the German
operations plans and attack orders, [however], make it crystal clear that. . .the main effort of
Marneschutz-Reims was on the left flank of the Seventh Army, which was supposed to envelop
Reims from the west, while the First Army attacked to envelop the city from the east.
